Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

InboxAI ("we", "our", "us") is a Chrome extension that helps you organize Gmail. This policy explains exactly what we collect, why, and where it goes — written to match what the extension actually does, not a generic template.

1. What we collect

  • Your Gmail address — used to identify your account and track your scan usage and subscription status.
  • A Google authorization token — generated when you sign in, used only to read and label your Gmail on your behalf. It is stored in our database so you don't need to sign in again on every scan.
  • Email metadata, only during a scan — subject line, sender address, a short preview snippet, and two headers (List-Unsubscribe, Precedence). This is used in memory to classify each email and is not saved to our database.
  • Basic account/usage data — how many scans you've used today, and whether your account is on a free or premium plan.
  • Payment confirmation — when you upgrade, your email address is passed to our payment processor (Razorpay) to associate the payment with your account. We do not see or store your card or UPI details — Razorpay handles that directly.

We do not read, store, or retain the full content or body of your emails at any point.

2. How we use it

  • To classify emails into labels (Promotions, Newsletters, Security, Finance, and so on) when you run a scan.
  • To apply or remove Gmail labels and archive emails you choose to clean up.
  • To enforce free-tier scan limits and track premium status.
  • If you're on Premium and request an AI explanation for a flagged email, the subject, sender, and preview snippet for that one email are sent to OpenAI to generate the explanation. This only happens when you explicitly ask for it.

3. Who we share data with

  • Google — for sign-in and to access your Gmail via the Gmail API.
  • Supabase — our database provider, where your email address, authorization token, and account/usage status are stored.
  • Razorpay — our payment processor, for handling subscription payments.
  • OpenAI — only for Premium users who request an AI explanation of a specific flagged email, and only for that email's metadata.

We do not sell your data, and we do not share it with advertisers or any party not listed above.

4. Data security

  • All traffic between the extension and our server is sent over HTTPS.
  • Your authorization token is stored in our database, which is encrypted at rest by our hosting provider.
  • Access to the database is restricted to our backend service — it is not publicly readable.

5. Your rights

  • You can revoke InboxAI's access to your Gmail at any time from your Google Account permissions page.
  • You can request deletion of your account data — including your authorization token and usage history — by emailing us below.

6. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in a way that affects what we collect or how we use it, we'll update the date at the top of this page.

Terms of Service

Last updated: June 2026

By installing or using InboxAI, you agree to these terms. If you don't agree, please don't use the extension.

1. What InboxAI does

InboxAI connects to your Gmail account (with your permission) to classify, label, and optionally archive emails based on automated rules. It does not send emails, delete anything permanently, or take any action you haven't triggered yourself by clicking Scan or Clean up.

2. Free vs. Premium

  • Free: 5 scans per day, up to 50 emails per scan, smart labels, basic phishing detection.
  • Premium: Unlimited scans, up to 200 emails per scan, bulk cleanup of 500+ emails at once, AI-generated explanations for flagged emails, advanced analytics.

3. Payments and refunds

  • Premium is billed monthly through Razorpay, in INR (₹99/mo) or USD ($1.19/mo) for international users.
  • Payments are non-refundable once processed. You can stop paying at any time by not renewing; there's no lock-in contract.
  • We may change prices in the future. If we do, we'll display the new price before you pay — your existing billing period won't change retroactively.

4. Your responsibilities

  • You're responsible for keeping your own Google account secure.
  • You won't use InboxAI to access, label, or archive email on an account that isn't yours without permission.
  • You won't attempt to abuse, overload, or reverse-engineer the service in a way that disrupts it for other users.

5. Automated classification isn't perfect

InboxAI's phishing and category detection is automated and rule-based. It can occasionally mislabel an email or miss something it should have flagged. Don't rely on it as your only safeguard against phishing — use your own judgment, especially for anything involving money or credentials.

6. Termination

We may suspend or terminate access for accounts that violate these terms, abuse the service, or attempt to circumvent scan limits. You can stop using InboxAI at any time by uninstalling the extension and revoking access from your Google Account.
